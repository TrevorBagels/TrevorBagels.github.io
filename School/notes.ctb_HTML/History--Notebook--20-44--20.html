Bus Boycotts 1940 – before CRM
Major form of protest against south african authorities prior to 1948
Causes of the boycotts were more economic than political
Not planned protests, they were a popular reaction to the decision of various bus companies
Boycotts were closely related to the extremely low wages paid
positives/negatives
+ Once a boycott began, committees were formed on behalf of the poor commuters who couldn’t afford the fare increase
+ Boycotts were often successful, but had limitations
- Could not be initiated by AN or COP
- Required bus companies to raise fares
- Could not dictate timing of any protests
Alexandra Bus Boycott (1957)
Began in January due to raise of fares from the township into the city, the size of the boycott indicated that there was more to be upset about other than the increase of bus fares.
The media covered the boycott and the world started to pay attention to the problems
This was viewed as a major thread by the apartheid government
In what ways was the Alexandra Bus Boycott of 1957 a success?
It received attention from the media, which spread awareness about the problems in South Africa.
